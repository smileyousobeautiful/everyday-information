## 📄 文章
The 2025-2030 DGAs emphasize high-quality protein, healthy fats, fruits, vegetables, and whole grains, while discouraging highly processed foods and refined carbs.

Protein remains a priority, with consumers focusing on its role in muscle maintenance, weight management, and overall health. But innovative protein sources like lab-grown meat and plant-based options are gaining traction.

According to Harvard Professor Frank Hu, "The new Guidelines move in the right direction by reinforcing the importance of reducing added sugars and cutting back on refined grains and other highly processed foods."

Key recommendations:
- Increase protein intake to 0.54 to 0.73 grams per pound of body weight
- More fruits, vegetables, and whole grains
- Limit added sugars and refined carbs
- Sustainable protein sources

The focus is on balanced nutrition, not just protein. The guidelines recommend:
- Vegetables: 3 servings per day
- Fruits: 2 servings per day
- Healthy fats from whole foods
