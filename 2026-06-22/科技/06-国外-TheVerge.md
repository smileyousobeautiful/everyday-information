## 📄 文章
Samsung is no longer South Korea's most valuable company.

Chipmaker SK Hynix, which supplies high-bandwidth memory (HBM) chips to Nvidia and Google, has overtaken Samsung, with its market capitalization now hitting $1.35 trillion.

Samsung had held the top spot since 2000, prior to the AI boom.

This marks a significant shift in the tech industry, driven by the AI boom. SK Hynix's HBM chips are essential for AI accelerators, and the demand has skyrocketed as AI companies race to build more powerful computing infrastructure.

The rise of SK Hynix reflects the broader transformation of the semiconductor industry. Memory chips, once considered a commodity, have become critical AI infrastructure.

This is a wake-up call for Samsung, which has been slow to capitalize on the AI opportunity. The company needs to reorient its strategy to focus on AI chips.
