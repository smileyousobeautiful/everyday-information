# Post-Traumatic Growth: Finding Meaning After Adversity

## 📋 Basic Info
- **Source**: Psychology Today
- **Stage**: Collection/Analysis/Writing/Review

## 💡 Core Insight
Many trauma survivors experience positive psychological change, not just suffering.

## 📖 Background
Tedeschi and Calhoun coined "post-traumatic growth" (PTG) in 1996. Research shows 50-70% of trauma survivors report some degree of positive change. This occurs across five domains: personal strength, new possibilities, relating to others, appreciation of life, and spiritual development.

## 📄 Article
Five domains of PTG:
1. Enhanced personal strength
2. Recognition of new possibilities
3. Deeper relationships
4. GreaterLife appreciation
5. Spiritual/existential change

## 🔄 Second-Order Effect
Trauma-informed therapy will emphasize growth potential.

## 🔄 Reverse View
Growth narratives shouldn't pressure survivors to "find the silver lining."

## 🎯 Scenario Planning
3 months to process initial trauma; 6 months to begin meaning-making; 1 year to recognize growth signs.

## 🚀 Action Suggestions
1. Allow time for grief before searching for meaning
2. Connect with support communities
3. Consider therapy trained in PTG approaches
