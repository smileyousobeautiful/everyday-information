## 📄 文章
Global oil markets experienced significant volatility on March 30, 2026, as prices surged past $100 per barrel benchmark.

The surge was driven by escalating Middle East tensions. U.S. President Trump issued threats against Iranian oil infrastructure, stating that if Iran does not accept a peace agreement and reopen the Strait of Hormuz, the U.S. would destroy the Khark Island oil hub and associated facilities.

The Strait of Hormuz handles approximately one-third of global seaborne oil trade, making it a critical chokepoint for global energy supply. Any disruption could have severe consequences for the world economy.

Market analysts warn that if tensions further escalate, energy supplies faces greater disruption risk. The situation has also prompted concerns about global food security, as the region is a key source for fertilizer exports.

The United Nations Trade and Development Conference warned that transit through Hormuz has dropped by over 95%, causing significant disruptions to energy and fertilizer transport, leading to rising prices in these sectors.
