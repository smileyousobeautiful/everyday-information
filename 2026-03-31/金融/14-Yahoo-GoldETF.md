## 📄 文章
On March 30, 2026, major gold ETFs saw significant outflows as investors' risk appetite improved.

SPDR Gold Trust, the world's largest gold-backed ETF, saw holdings decline by 3.43 tons, bringing total holdings to 1,046.13 tons. This represents a notable shift from the safe-haven flows seen in recent months.

iShares Silver Trust experienced even larger outflows, with holdings declining by 121.10 tons to 15,288.36 tons. The decline in silver ETF holdings suggests investors are reducing exposure to precious metals.

Analysts attribute this shift to several factors: improving economic sentiment, rising stock markets, and reduced geopolitical tensions. When risk assets become more attractive, investors often reduce gold positions.

Despite short-term outflows, many analysts believe gold retains long-term value as a portfolio hedge. The yellow metal has historically performed well during periods of economic uncertainty.
