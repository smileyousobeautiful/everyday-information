## 📄 文章
I was terrified my first time. I imagined lying on a couch, crying while a stern therapist takes notes. The reality couldn't be more different.

Here's what actually happens:
First session is intake. Therapist asks about your history, current challenges, what brought you in. Think of it as getting on the same page.

Most therapy happens face-to-face, talking. There's no couch—I sat in a comfortable chair, therapist sat across from me. Some therapists use worksheets, some guide conversations, some teach specific skills. It depends on their approach.

You'll likely do homework. Yes, therapy has homework! Things to practice between sessions. This is where real change happens.

It's collaborative. The therapist guides, but YOU do the work. It's not passive—your engagement matters.

How do you find the right therapist?
- Credentials matter (licensed, trained)
- Specialization—what are they good at?
- Trust your gut—if it doesn't feel right, try another
- First sessions are often consultations—You're interviewing THEM

Common misconceptions debunked:
"You need to have a serious problem" - FALSE. Therapy is for anyone wanting growth.
"CBT is the only evidence-based" - FALSE. Many approaches work.
"I'll be judged" - A good therapist creates non-judgment space.
"I should know what to say" - Therapeutguides the process.

The bottom line: If you're considering it, just try. Many clinics offer sliding scale fees. Online therapy expands access. Don't let stigma or fear stop you.
