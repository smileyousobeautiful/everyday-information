睡眠与心理健康之间存在双向关系——睡眠问题会导致心理问题，心理问题也会导致睡眠障碍。改善睡眠往往是心理健康干预的第一步。

## 📄 文章
Can't sleep? Watch your mood worsen. Depressed? Watch your sleep suffer. It's a vicious cycle—and breaking it is crucial for mental health.

The science is undeniable:
-One night of poor sleep increases anxiety by 30%
-Chronic insomnia triples depression risk
-Sleep deprivation impairs emotional regulation in the prefrontal cortex
-Glial "washing" of brain toxins happens during deep sleep

The relationship is bidirectional: Poor sleep causes mental health decline; mental health conditions cause poor sleep. This creates a feedback loop that's hard to break.

Sleep improvement strategies:
1. Consistency: Same bedtime and wake time, even weekends
2. Darkness: Melatonin needs darkness to release
3. Cool: Body temp drops during sleep—keep room 65-68°F
4. No screens 1 hour before bed: Blue light suppresses melatonin
5. wind-down routine: Signal your brain it's time
6. Avoid caffeine 8 hours before bed
7. Exercise—but not too late

For those whose sleep problems stem from anxiety or depression, addressing the underlying condition is necessary. Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia (CBT-I) is highly effective and addresses both the behaviors and thoughts around sleep.
