## 📖 背景信息
Reddit r/psychology 社区关于"冒名顶替综合症"的讨论热度持续不减。这是一个由心理学家 Pauline Rose Clance 于1978年提出的概念，用于描述那些achievements明明很成功但内心一直觉得自己是骗子的情况。

这种现象在职场精英、学者、艺术家中尤为普遍。研究估计，高达70%的人在其职业生涯中至少经历过一次冒名顶替综合症的感受。

Imposter Syndrome: The Secret Struggle of High Achievers

## 📄 文章
You've worked hard to get here. You've achieved things most people only dream of. Yet, when praise comes your way, you dismiss it. "They don't really know me." "It was luck." "Anybody could have done it."

Welcome to imposter syndrome—the hidden battle of people who objectively succeed but internally feel like frauds.

Why does it happen? Several factors contribute: Perfectionism—"If I'm not perfect, I'm a failure." Family dynamics—being praised for achievement rather than effort. Membership in underrepresented groups—the added pressure of representation. Fields that emphasize innate talent over growth.

The dark irony: Imposter syndrome often hits high performers hardest. Because you set unrealistically high standards, meeting them feels like the minimum; falling short feels catastrophic.

Ways to combat it: 
-Gather evidence: List your wins, specifically. Look at feedback, metrics, outcomes.
-Challenge the inner critic: Would you say this to a friend?
-Talk about it: Most people have experienced it—you're not alone.
-Redefine success: Effort and growth, not perfection.
-Therapy: Can help rewire these thought patterns.

The truth: Doubt is normal. Success is real. You belong where you are.
Your inner critic doesn't speak for reality—it speaks for fear.
