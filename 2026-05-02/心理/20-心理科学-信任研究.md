## 📄 文章
A 2025 study in Psychological Bulletin found that people who are generally more trusting tend to report higher levels of subjective well-being.

World Happiness Report 2025 highlights that people are much kinder than we expect. Despite the pessimism we often hear about, research shows that kindness and trust significantly boost happiness.

People with higher levels of trust—in both others and institutions—report higher levels of subjective well-being, an important indicator of quality of life and predictor of longevity.
