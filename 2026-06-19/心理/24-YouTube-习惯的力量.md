## 📄 文章
What if you could be 37 times better by the end of the year? Not by trying harder—but by being just 1% better every day.

James Clear's "Atomic Habits" provides the roadmap.

The power of 1%:

If you improve by 1% every day for one year, you're 37 times better. But if you get 1% worse every day, you decline to nearly zero.

This is the power of habits—they compound.

The four laws of habit change:

First, make it obvious. Design your environment for the behavior you want.

Second, make it attractive. Pair the habit with something you enjoy.

Third, make it easy. Reduce friction for good habits, increase friction for bad ones.

Fourth, make it satisfying. Immediate rewards reinforce the habit.

The two-minute rule:

When you start a new habit, it should take less than two minutes. "Read" becomes "read one page." "Exercise" becomes "put on workout clothes."

Identity-based habits:

The most powerful driver of behavior is identity. Instead of "I want to run a marathon," become "I am a runner." Instead of "I want to read more," become "I am a reader."

Environment design:

Make good habits easy and bad habits hard. Keep fruits on the counter, junk food in the pantry. Put your running shoes by the door.

Habit stacking:

Link a new habit to an existing one. "After I pour my morning coffee, I will meditate for 2 minutes."
