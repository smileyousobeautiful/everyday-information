## 📄 文章
Your heart is racing. Your breathing is shallow. Your mind is spinning. You're sure something terrible is about to happen. But nothing is happening. This is anxiety.

Anxiety is your body's threat system misfiring. It evolved to protect you from predators. But in modern life, there are few real predators—so your system goes off for work presentations, social situations, and uncertain futures.

Understanding anxiety:

First, anxiety is not danger—it's the alarm. The feeling is real, but the danger usually isn't.

Second, anxiety will pass. It has a natural arc—it peaks and then subsides.

Third, avoidance makes it worse. Avoiding what you fear reinforces the fear.

Evidence-based strategies:

First, deep breathing. Breathe in for 4 counts, hold for 4, out for 4. This activates the parasympathetic nervous system.

Second, cognitive defusion. Instead of "I'm going to fail," say "I'm having the thought that I'm going to fail." This creates distance from the thought.

Third, exposure. Gradually face what you fear. Each exposure weakens the fear.

Fourth, exercise. Physical activity uses up the stress hormones.

Fifth, challenge thoughts. Ask: "What's the evidence?" "What's another explanation?"

Sixth, mindfulness. Observe anxiety without fighting it. "I notice I'm feeling anxious."
