## 📄 文章
What makes relationships last? Dr. John Gottman has spent decades studying couples. His findings show what works—and what doesn't.

The Sound Relationship House:

First, love maps. Do you know your partner's inner world—dreams, fears, hopes, stresses? The more you know, the stronger the bond.

Second, fondness and admiration. Do you genuinely like and respect your partner? This is the foundation.

Third, turning toward. When your partner reaches out, do you respond? These small moments matter more than grand gestures.

Fourth, the "bids" for connection. Your partner constantly bids for connection. Do you turn toward, away, or against?

Conflict is inevitable. But it's how you manage it that matters:

First, soften startup. Start conversations gently. "I feel ___ because ___"

Second, repair attempts. "I'm sorry. Let's try again."

Third, accept influence. Let your partner's perspective matter to you.

Fourth, compromise. Find solutions that work for both.

Fifth, tolerate your partner's flaws. No one is perfect.

The 5:1 ratio:

Gottman found that stable relationships have 5 positive interactions for every negative one. Nurture positivity.

Daily habits that strengthen relationships:

First, share something new every day.

Second, ask about your partner's day.

Third, physically affectionate—hugs, kisses, touches.

Fourth, appreciation—express gratitude daily.

Fifth, touch without sex—non-sexual physical connection.
