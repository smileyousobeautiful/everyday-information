## 📄 文章
You might think of sleep as a passive state—nothing happening, just rest. But research shows sleep is one of the most active processes in your body. It's not downtime—it's maintenance time.

Why sleep matters:

MEMORY: Sleep is when your brain processes and stores memories. During REM sleep, emotional memories are processed. During deep sleep, facts and skills are consolidated. Poor sleep = poor memory.

EMOTIONAL REGULATION: After one night of poor sleep, the prefrontal cortex (reasoning) is less active, while the amygdala (emotional reactivity) is more active. This is why poor sleep makes you irritable and reactive.

IMMUNE FUNCTION: During sleep, your immune system produces cytokines and antibodies. Poor sleep = weaker immunity.

METABOLISM: Sleep affects hormones that control hunger. Poor sleep increases ghrelin (hunger hormone) and decreases leptin (satiety hormone). This is why poor sleep leads to weight gain.

CLEARING TOXINS: During deep sleep, your brain's glymphatic system activates, clearing out metabolic waste products including beta-amyloid (linked to Alzheimer's).

The sleep cycle:

A full cycle is about 90 minutes: light sleep → deep sleep → REM sleep. You go through 4-6 cycles per night.

Deep sleep (stages 3-4): Physical restoration, growth hormone release, immune strengthening.

REM sleep: Emotional processing, memory consolidation, dreaming.

How to improve sleep:

First, consistent schedule. Same wake time, same sleep time, even on weekends.

Second, dark and cool. Bedroom should be dark (use blackout curtains) and cool (65-68°F/18-20°C).

Third, no caffeine 6 hours before bed. Caffeine has a half-life of 5-6 hours.

Fourth, no screens 1 hour before bed. Blue light suppresses melatonin.

Fifth, exercise—but not too close to bedtime.

Sixth, wind down routine. Dim lights, relaxing activities.

Seventh, don't force sleep. If you can't sleep, get up and do something boring until sleepy.
