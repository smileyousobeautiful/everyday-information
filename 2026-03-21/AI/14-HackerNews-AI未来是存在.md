## 📝 English Subtitle
AI's Future Is "Presence" Not More Models

## 📄 文章
There's a lot of noise in the AI industry about faster models, bigger GPUs, and new benchmarks. But stepping back, 2026 will be defined by something simpler and harder to engineer: presence.

The concept of "presence" in AI refers to the quality of interaction that makes people feel truly connected and understood. It's about building AI systems that can engage in ways that feel authentically human, even across distances.

This shift represents an important maturation of the AI industry. For years, the focus has been on technical metrics: parameter counts, benchmark scores, processing speed. While these improvements matter, they don't necessarily translate to better user experiences.

The future of AI isn't about adding more capabilities to existing paradigms. It's about fundamentally rethinking how humans and AI interact. The goal should be creating systems that people genuinely want to use, not just impressive demos.

Key aspects of presence include: understanding context and nuance, maintaining coherent conversations over time, recognizing and responding to emotions appropriately, and building genuine rapport with users.

This approach has significant implications for AI development. Rather than pursuing ever-larger models, researchers and developers should focus on understanding what makes human interaction meaningful and translating those insights into AI systems.

The challenges are substantial. Presence is difficult to measure and optimize. It requires deep understanding of human psychology, communication, and social dynamics. But the potential rewards are significant - AI that truly connects with users could transform everything from education to healthcare to entertainment.

As the AI industry matures, expect to see more discussion about presence and less about raw technical capabilities. The best AI of 2026 won't necessarily be the biggest or fastest - it will be the most human.
