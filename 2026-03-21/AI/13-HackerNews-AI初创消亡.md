## 📝 English Subtitle
99% of AI Startups Will Die by 2026

## 📄 文章
The AI startup landscape in 2026 is facing unprecedented challenges. According to discussions on Hacker News, a significant majority of AI startups will not survive the year.

The reasons for this high failure rate are multifaceted. First, the barriers to entry for AI development have decreased dramatically. With open-source models becoming more accessible, anyone with basic technical skills can build AI-powered applications. This has led to an explosion of AI startups, creating intense competition.

Second, many AI startups lack clear business models. While they may have impressive technology, translating that into sustainable revenue remains challenging. Enterprise customers are demanding proven ROI, and VC funding is becoming more selective.

Third, the cost of running AI businesses is high. Infrastructure costs, talent acquisition, and ongoing model training require significant capital. Many startups burn through funding faster than they can generate revenue.

Fourth, market saturation is becoming a serious issue. With so many players in the AI space, differentiation is increasingly difficult. Startups offering similar services compete primarily on price, which is unsustainable.

However, this "die-off" is not necessarily negative. It represents a natural market correction that will separate viable businesses from hype-driven ventures. The survivors will be those with genuine competitive advantages, whether in technology, domain expertise, or business model.

The implications for the broader AI ecosystem are significant. Resources will consolidate around stronger players. Talent may become more available as failed startups release workers back into the market. Innovation may slow as fewer resources flow to speculative ventures.

For aspiring entrepreneurs, the message is clear: building an AI startup requires more than just impressive technology. A sustainable business model and clear path to profitability are essential.
