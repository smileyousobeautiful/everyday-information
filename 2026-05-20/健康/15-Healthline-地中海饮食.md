## 📄 文章
Search for "best diet for heart health" and you'll find endless options claiming superiority. But when serious scientists want the evidence, they keep returning to the same answer: Mediterranean diet.

The landmark PREDIMED trial enrolled over 7,000 participants at high cardiovascular risk, randomly assigning them to Mediterranean diet with extra virgin olive oil, Mediterranean diet with nuts, or control diet. After 5 years, both Mediterranean groups showed approximately 30% reduction in major cardiovascular events - strokes, heart attacks, and cardiovascular deaths. Such dramatic results explain why this keeps appearing at the top of recommendations.

What makes Mediterranean eating so effective? The eating pattern emphasizes abundant vegetables, fruits, whole grains, legumes, nuts, seeds, and olive oil. Fish appears regularly while red meat stays occasional. Processed foods and added sugars are minimal. No food groups are eliminated - instead, healthy foods take center stage.

Key mechanisms explaining cardiovascular protection include improved lipid profiles, reduced inflammation markers, better blood pressure control, and improved endothelial function. Extra virgin olive oil provides polyphenols with antioxidant effects. Omega-3 fatty acids from fish reduce triglycerides and stabilize heart rhythms. Fiber from plants lowers LDL cholesterol absorption.

The sustainability advantage matters significantly. Keto and very-low-carb diets often show initial success but prove difficult to maintain long-term. The Mediterranean approach offers flexibility and pleasure - wine with meals, shared dining, moderate indulgence - making these patterns genuinely adoptable for decades.

Implementation remains practical. Stock extra virgin olive oil for dressings and light cooking. Prioritize vegetables at every meal. Swap refined grains for whole versions. Enjoy fish twice weekly. Nuts make excellent snacks. Red meat becomes occasional rather than central.

The lifestyle component extends to how meals are consumed. Shared family meals, mindful eating, reduced screen time during meals - all traditional practices aligning with modern research on mindful consumption patterns supporting metabolic health.
