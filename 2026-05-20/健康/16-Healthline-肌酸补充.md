## 📄 Article
Walk into any健身房 and you'll likely find creatine in someone's shelf. This simple compound consisting of three amino acids has accumulated more positive research than ANY other supplement, making it the undisputed champion of legal ergogenic aids.

Mechanisms involve ATP regeneration. During high-intensity exercise lasting under 10 seconds, your body rapidly depletes ATP. Creatine helps regenerate ATP faster, effectively giving you more explosive energy for reps and sets. The result: more volume lifted over time, which directly correlates with greater muscle growth.

Over 500 peer-reviewed studies confirm these effects. Meta-analyses show approximately 8-15% improvement in strength performance and 2-5 pounds greater lean mass gain over weeks compared to placebo. No other marketed supplement comes close to replicating these consistent results.

What's surprising is emerging cognitive research. The brain uses enormous energy, and creatine supplementation appears to support cognitive function during mentally demanding tasks, especially with sleep restriction. For students pulling all-nighters, elderly experiencing cognitive decline, or anyone facing cognitively demanding situations, this offers intriguing potential.

Common misconceptions persist though. Creatine doesn't cause kidney damage in healthy individuals - extensive studies rule this out. Water retention occurs intracellularly ( inside muscle cells ), actually making muscles appear fuller rather than causing dangerous bloating. Cycling off isn't necessary - sustained use maintains benefits.

Dosing typically starts with 20g daily ( 5g four times ) for a week loading phase, then 3-5g daily maintenance. Taking with carbohydrates slightly improves absorption due to insulin-mediated uptake. Many find taking post-workout with carbohydrates optimal.

Quality variations exist despite simplicity. Third-party testing ( Informed Sport, NSF Certified for Sport ) verifies purity and contamination-free products. Generic monohydrate performs identically to expensive designer versions.
