## 📄 article
"Inflammation" has become meaningless buzzword—everything seems to cause or cure it. Reality lies between complexity: chronic systemic inflammation genuinely underlies modern disease patterns, and food demonstrably modulates inflammation through multiple pathways.

Acute inflammation represents protective response—forgetting, tripping, cutting triggers transient inflammatory cascade enabling tissue repair. The danger lies in chronic elevation—a persistent low-level inflammatory state failing to resolve. This drives atherosclerosis, cancer progression, metabolic dysfunction, neurodegenerative processes.

Omega-3 to omega-6 ratio matters fundamentally. Modern Western diets deliver excessive omega-6 from vegetable oils (soy, corn, sunflower) in processed foods. Omega-3 deficiency means resolvins—the signaling molecules resolving inflammation—cannot synthesis adequately. Increasing fatty fish intake addresses this directly.

Polyphenols from colorful plants exhibit anti-inflammatory effects through multiple mechanisms: berries, dark leafy greens, green tea, turmeric, dark chocolate. Distinguishing whole food intake from extract supplements shows better outcomes—food matrix interactions exceed single-molecule effects.

Gut microbiome relationship matters significantly. Fiber-fermented by gut bacteria produces anti-inflammatory short-chain fatty acids like butyrate. Dysbiosis and fiber deficiency thus allow inflammation persistence even with low inflammatory diets—the gut-sourced resolution fails.

Foods triggering inflammatory response individually vary. Standard Western diet testing shows processed foods, refined carbohydrates, and excessive omega-6 promote inflammation even with salad dressing claims. Elimination trials reveal individual response variation some have significant sensitivities requiring personalization.

Nightshade controversy persists without strong support—at most minimal inflammatory effect for susceptible individuals. Histamine intolerance from improperly stored fish or aged cheese produces mast-cell activation mimicking inflammation. Trial-elimination rather than categorical avoidance guides personal adaptation.

Anti-inflammatory breakfast example: berries with walnuts and grounded flaxseed, green tea, olive oil on whole grain toast—this delivers omega-3, polyphenols, fiber, and anti-oxidants working concertedly.

Long-term adherence requires practical implementation: building meals around vegetables using cooking fat replacements for processed oils, flavor abundance from herbs and spices rather than salt, and acceptance finding meals delicious satisfying rather than deprivation.
